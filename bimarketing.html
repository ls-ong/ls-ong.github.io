Berkeley Innovation
Marketing Lead, External VP, President —2017 to 2018

HCD Design Conference

In Spring 2018, Berkeley Innovation put on our annual HCD Design Conference, bringing in a range of industry speakers on campus to talk design. This year, our theme was Making Meaning — how design is always charged with epistemic consequences. In addition to helping put on the event, I also created the marketing materials. My goal was to communicate the formation of meaningful concepts from a chaotic inventory of non-meaningful components. This came off rather abstract, with the design elements held in tension, in formation.

Recruitment

In building a recognizable brand and message for Berkeley Innovation's Fall 2017 recruitment season, I worked alongside Karen Chou, aiming to develop a consistent theme supported by a set of design assets.

Our goal was to convey a sense of community — how thinkers, designers, tinkerers of all stripes come together and belong at Berkeley Innovation. This manifested quite literally, with our logo reimagined as a three dimensional space, depicting members interacting and acting in various steps of the design process: ideating, iterating, interviewing.

Completed Flyer

With the designs that we created for the flyer, I built out a series of vector assets that would be used across our social media channels — for instance, a cover photo.

Cover photo for Facebook event

These assets were also used on the Fall 2017 Recruitment website, coded by our Director of Tech, Kim.
